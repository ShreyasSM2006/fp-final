Who We Are

Focused on its vision, Vatsalya Trust Mumbai creates lasting solutions to improve the quality of life for destitute and deprived individuals across all ages, race, caste, religion, and gender.

We provide comprehensive services including Adoption, Orphanage care, Old Age Home support, and care for destitute girls — truly living up to the name “Vatsalya”, meaning affection.

Our Journey

Vatsalya Trust Mumbai was founded on 8th February 1983 and registered under the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950.

The founders — Smt. Sanjivani Raykar, Late Shri G.A. Damle, Late Dr. Shivram Athavale, Shri Nijasure, and other philanthropists — envisioned a dedicated organization for the care and rehabilitation of orphaned and abandoned children.

What We Do

  • Adoption services for orphaned, abandoned, and surrendered children
  • Care for infants received from hospitals, police, and public places
  • Orphanage and childcare support
  • Old age home services
  • Protection and care for destitute girls

Our Impact

Around 1275 children have found loving homes through our adoption programs.

Many of these children continue to stay connected as volunteers and donors, creating a powerful cycle of care and support.

A milestone moment: The 1201st infant was adopted on 8th January 2021 by a family from Tamil Nadu.

Recognition & Certifications

  • Recognized by Central Adoption Resource Authority (CARA)
  • FCRA Registered
  • Gold Certification by GuideStar India
  • ISO 9001:2015 Certified (TUV Austria)
  • Appreciated by India Development and Relief Fund (IDRF)

Where We Work

Our projects are located across Maharashtra at Kanjurmarg, Sanpada, Alibag, and Badlapur.

We actively collaborate with local NGOs to reach remote tribal communities, including:

  • Mauli Shikshan Sanstha (Palghar)
  • Shabari Seva Samiti (Nandurbar)
  • Sneha Samaj (Talasari)
  • Parivartan Mahila Sanstha (Mokhada)

Our Growth

From a small initiative in 1983, Vatsalya Trust Mumbai has grown into a strong organization, expanding its reach and impact across multiple regions and communities.

Today, it stands as a symbol of compassion, built on strong roots of Love, Affection, and Care.
